About Me

I'm currently on a journey to wrap that passion in actual talent and technical skills. I've spent the entirety of the pandemic (2020-2021) learning and applying what I've learned to push myself further. I have been able to use this time to devote myself entirely to my goal. I completed two bootcamps, back to back, one of them being full-time. I’ve had the opportunity to experience two amazing programs and connect with some really great mentors, and I’m now more ready to enter the field once more.

My tech obsession started early, cementing itself when I got my first computer at the age of seven. Without internet access, I instead used it to practice HTML from a tattered secondhand guidebook. I built myself a basic diary with guaranteed privacy since no one else in my house had any idea how to use the computer. I did it for fun; I didn’t know it was possible that these skills could be a career.

I didn’t know how much I didn’t know. There were a lot of false starts between that first computer and now. I followed leads at jobs that weren’t for me. I took classes in subjects that didn’t interest me. I have a non-traditional schooling background because most of my younger years were spent chasing goals I wasn’t passionate about.

In 2018, I had a shot to break into the tech field. That taste unlocked what seemed to have been missing: my ability to believe in myself. For the first time, I was excited to go to work. I was excited to share knowledge with peers. It was a great learning opportunity, and while it wasn’t the right fit for me at the time, it showed me what was possible and gave me the drive to work toward a career in what I love.

Since that position, I have spent as much time as I can learning as much as I could. LaunchCode was a great way to teach me how to learn again. Though incredibly tough, the lower time commitment allowed my brain to adjust to being focused and involved in a subject once more. I completed LC101 in April 2020.

Friday, March 12, 2020, I completed my last day of SkilledKC’s full-time software development cohort. It was demanding. It was akin to a full-time job. It was harder than anything else I’ve tried. I’m very fortunate that I was even able to pursue this opportunity. I recognize that it’s not the norm as an adult to have an extra 50 hours a week to dedicate to learning. I loved the small class size, which allowed for a more intimate and inclusive setting. An incredibly patient, encouraging teacher that pushes you to figure things out, but is there when you need help, has been an invaluable resource. I’ve come away from SkilledKC not only knowing more than I ever have in my years of self-study, but also with a newfound confidence in skills that I’d only daydreamed of before.
